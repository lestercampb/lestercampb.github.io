About

All in.
It’s the way I’ve always lived.

In school I put all into my school work and leadership activities. I was just one of sixty who graduated from the Business Honors program at Indiana University in 2005. I put all into studying for the CPA exam and passed all four parts on my first attempt. I put all into my career as a public accountant, and over the years, with a mix of corporate and public accounting experience, I worked my way up to Senior Manager at Ernst & Young, a worldwide, Big Four accounting firm. There, I was all in, just a few years from Partner, when I realized Partner was not a position that would fulfill me on a personal level. With three small children at home, I decided to go all in on them, and quit my demanding but rewarding job cold turkey for a life as a stay at home mom.

Over the next years I went all in with my children and myself, training for and running a mini marathon, becoming “mommy monster” on the playground, teaching music for Vacation Bible School, and spending time on the golf course. Throughout all this I have reflected on my career and determined the parts of my old career that I enjoyed the most – the logical thinking, mental challenge, client service, leading, and teaching, can be found in another industry with a different end path – Software development. So, I decided to go all in again and participate in a 12 week immersive JavaScript bootcamp at Eleven Fifty.

I know I have much to learn, and in many ways will be starting over, but I also know I have the drive and determination to succeed, the experience to know when to ask questions, and a love for learning a new skillset. Accountants see strength in companies with great assets. Could I be an asset to your team?
